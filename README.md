# ✨ Aluré



## 🧠 Overview
Aluré is a college-focused AI styling platform that helps users generate outfits based on their wardrobe, personal preferences, weather, and local events. It delivers personalized outfit suggestions that continuously improve through user feedback.

The average college student is constantly balancing classes, work, and social life—while still wanting to make the most of their college experience by attending events and staying involved. Aluré helps take one thing off their plate by making outfit decisions effortless, so they can focus on showing up and enjoying the moment.

---

## 💡 Competitors
We wanted to solve a real, everyday problem:  
**“I have a class soon, but I don’t know what to wear.”**

While existing AI fashion apps like Alta provide personalized outfit recommendations, weather-based styling, and wardrobe organization, they are designed for a broad audience and often emphasize shopping and product recommendations.

Aluré takes a different approach. It is built specifically for college students and focuses on making the most out of what you already own.

We focus on:
- Campus and local events (parties, games, concerts)
- Fast, low-effort outfit decisions for busy schedules  
- Styling based entirely on your existing wardrobe  
- Helping students stay involved without added stress  

College students already have a lot to balance, but they also want to make the most of their time—going out, attending events, and being present. Aluré helps by removing one daily stressor, making it easier to get ready and show up with confidence.

# How to Run ALURÉ

## What You Need First (One-Time Install)
- **Node.js** — download the LTS version  
- **Git**  
- **Expo Go** on your phone — App Store or Google Play  

## Step 1 — Clone and Install
```bash
git clone https://github.com/Markboon123/Alure.git
cd Alure
npm install
```
## Step 2 — Start the app
Start the development server with:
```bash
npx expo start
```
A QR code will appear in your terminal.

## Step 3 — Open on your phone

- **iPhone:** Open the Camera app, point it at the QR code, then tap the banner that appears  
- **Android:** Open Expo Go, tap **Scan QR code**, and point it at the QR code  

> Make sure your phone and computer are on the **same Wi-Fi network**.  
> The app should load in about 10–20 seconds.
